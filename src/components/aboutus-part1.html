About

OguName.com

OguName is a web-based project to archive personal names within the ogu culture. As captured in the logo. 'name' is 'Yinko' (or 'oyin') in the gun language of ogu people of the West Africa coast. The project documents the meanings and roots of the names in English language.


OguName.com is part of a broader initiative to promote digital inclusion and preserve the gun language. It contributes to the documentation of various aspects of the people's cultural expression, identities, and history on the worldwide web.


Inspired by the YorubaName.com project and the pioneering work of the late ogu local historian, Chief Theophilus Ola Avoseh, OguName.com is a crowdsourced platform run by volunteer culture and language enthusiasts.


We rely on donations and support to sustain this initiative.
